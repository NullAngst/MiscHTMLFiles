LOST RECORDS

TAPE 3: THE FINAL CUT

A hypothetical conclusion. From the journal of Swann Holloway.

Part 1: The Echo of Memory

October 23, 2022

We sit in Nora’s old garage now. Twenty-seven years have passed, and the smell of dust, and damp concrete lingers in the air. Faded band posters are peeling from the walls, ghosts of a past we can suddenly, vividly, remember. The memories are back, not like a movie you watch, but like a dam breaking flooding two decades of carefully constructed emptiness. The smell of pine and gasoline. The sound of Corey’s scream. Kat’s face, pale and fierce in the eerie glow of the Abyss.

The air is thick with ghosts and the hum of a cheap space heater. It’s a strange paradox, to feel so much joy and so much horror at once. Nora is here. Really here. Autumn is curled on a couch in the house, finally asleep. We are all here, shipwrecked survivors on the shores of our own past. We’ve been talking for hours, filling in the gaps, stitching together the ragged edges of our lives. But every conversation circles the same black hole: the secret we buried, the pact we made.

"We were just kids," Autumn had whispered earlier, her voice raw. But we weren't, not at the end. The Abyss made sure of that.

Later, with the main house dark and quiet, Nora and I sit on her old couch, the one with the ripped cushion that was here even back then. The garage is our sanctuary. "I never stopped thinking about you," she says, her voice rough. "Even when I couldn't remember why. It was like... a phantom limb. I knew something was missing." I reach out, my fingers brushing against hers. It's hesitant, fragile. We aren't the girls from 1995 anymore. We are survivors, bound not just by a summer romance, but by a shared trauma that has defined us in its absence.

Part 2: The Beckoning

October 24, 2022

Sleep didn't come easy. The morning light filtering through the garage's grimy window feels accusatory. The first thing we did was pull the tape from the camcorder. The box it came in sits on the workbench like a strange, occult artifact. The old CRT and VCR were still setup, the garage frozen in time, a relic from another era. The hum and crackle as it powered on was a sound I hadn't heard in years.

We hooked up the camera, the three of us huddled together on the concrete floor. The screen flickered, rolling with static and familiar images of that summer. Our faces, so impossibly young. And then, at the end, after the footage of the cabin and us at the bar, the screen went black. For a moment, I thought it was over. But then, the static cleared, and a new image resolved. It was Kat. Her face, impossibly, was there. She was looking right at us, right *through* the lens. Her voice was a distorted whisper, strained through years and dimensions.

"It took me," the voice on the tape crackled. "But I'm not gone. I'm… in here. In the Abyss. It remembers. It holds on. You have to come back."

The silence that followed was heavier than anything I have ever felt. It wasn't a memory. It was a message. A call from beyond the grave. I looked at Nora, then at Autumn. The same terror was reflected in their eyes. "We have to go back," I said, my voice shaking. "To the clearing. To the Abyss."

Nora’s reaction was instant, visceral. "No. Absolutely not, Swann. We promised. She's gone." The fear in her eyes was the same fear I saw the night the cabin burned. It’s a wall she built to protect herself, and I was asking her to tear it down. Autumn was quieter, wrapping her arms around herself. "What if it's a trick?" she asked, her voice barely a whisper. "What if it wants the rest of us?"

Part 3: The Descent

Later that day

I came alone. I don’t know if that was brave or selfish. Nora kissed me before I left, a desperate, angry kiss that tasted of fear and regret. "Don't be a ghost," she said.

The woods are the same. Unchanged by time. My feet remember the way. And then I see it. It's not just a clearing. It's a wound in the world, shimmering with a soft, violet light. The Abyss. It’s open. It's been waiting.

My whole life, I’ve been the observer. The girl behind the camera, framing the world, keeping it at a safe distance. Documenting memories instead of living them. The camera is in my bag now, but I don't take it out. This isn't something to be recorded. It's something to be done.

A choice. The final one. For Kat. For us. For the girl who was too scared to be in the frame. I take a breath. And I step in.

Part 4: The Heart of the Rage

Temporal Stasis

There is no falling feeling. It’s more like… dissolving. My thoughts unravel into threads of color and sound. The air is cold, electric, and it whispers with a million forgotten moments. I am adrift in a sea of memory and emotion. I see moments from that summer, fractured and looping: the four of us laughing around a campfire, the image flickering and distorting into the flames of the burning cabin. I see Corey’s face, a silent, screaming ghost, reliving his final terror forever, his form made of pure fear. This place isn't just a prison made of feelings. It's an archive of pain, and it has a warden.

At the center of the swirling chaos, I find her. Kat. She's made of light and fury, woven into the very fabric of the Abyss. She isn't a ghost. She isn't alive. She is a constant, looping echo. A memory that refuses to fade. She turns to me, her form shimmering, her eyes burning with the same violet energy that powers this place.

"I knew you'd come," her voice is not a sound, but an echo inside my head, a mix of relief and sorrow. "I sent the box. I needed you to remember. To save me."

She shows me the truth of this place. The Abyss didn't exist before us. Our combined will, our desperate chant to save ourselves, opened a door. But it was her 'Rage' her furious, vibrant refusal to die that gave it a consciousness. It took Corey's life and our memories as payment, and it took her spirit as its anchor. She's been trapped here ever since, the heart of this place, forced to watch our stolen memories and Corey's last moments play on repeat for 27 years. She tried to run away after that night, live on her own terms, but was pulled here by a magnet she couldn't resist. To undo a bargain, a new one must be struck. The Abyss needs an anchor. It needs a new heart.

Part 5: Bloom & Let Go

Subjective Eternity.

The Abyss makes its offer. It speaks through Kat, but the voice is ancient and cold beneath hers. It doesn't show me a future; it builds a world around me. Suddenly, I'm in a sunlit apartment. Nora is there, laughing as she hands me a coffee. The trauma is gone. Autumn is visiting us, happy and healthy. There are no secrets, no ghosts. It is a perfect, seamless life, crafted from my deepest desires. The Abyss whispers the terms: all I have to do is accept this reality as my own. In this beautiful cage, I will live out this perfect story, and the S will feast on the potent emotions the love, the joy, the peace forever. I would be its heart, a willing battery, and Kat's echo would finally be allowed to fade.

It is so tempting. But I think of my camera. My desperate need to hit 'record', to capture every moment, to stop time from stealing the people and memories I love. This perfect life is just a distorted recording. A beautiful lie on an endless loop. It's the curse of the Abyss. It holds on. It never lets go. It feeds you what you want and eats your emotions until nothing is left, you're a shell, an echo. The only way to win is to do what it cannot: reject perfection, reject life, reject your desires.

"I will not accept your false reality, I will not be your savior." I say, and the beautiful illusion shatters, possible futures stream across the Abyss beckoning me to pick one. I turn to Kat's true form at the center of the madness, shimmering within the false reality. "Living a painful reality is better than a personal lie."

There is another way. Not to take her place, but to erase the her entirely. The Abyss is powered by Rage, Kat's rage at dying, our rage at Corey. It's fueled by the dark, looping trauma it is making Kat re-live. To kill it, we have to cut the power. We have to let go of the rage and forgive. "Kat," I plead with her echo, "You have to let go, too. Your emotions are chaining you here. We're here now. We remember you. You don't have to scream into the void anymore. You don't have to hurt anymore."

I close my eyes and focus past the illusion, past the pain. I focus on the 'Bloom' of that summer the real love, the real friendship. I accept the ending. I forgive us for being scared kids. I let the rage that has been a quiet poison in my soul for 27 years drain away, replacing it with the memory of Nora's hand in mine, jamming in her garage, talking with Autumn while she works, helping Kat enjoy her final moments in life. As my rage dies, the Abyss shrieks. It's starving. Kat can see my memories, they give her the strength to feel her own. The fury in her form softens, the burning starlight dims.

She smiles, a brilliant, supernova smile. The rage melts away, leaving only the friend I remember. "Tell Nora... to take care of you," her words are shouted into my mind, with more power than anything in the Abyss. With her rage gone, she is unchained, the Abyss it loses its consciousness. The whispers cease. The looping memories static out. The violet light folds in on itself, and the prison of pain demagnetizes, releasing me into the quiet dark.

Epilogue: The Found Footage

The future.

I woke up on the forest floor. The clearing was just a clearing. The air was crisp, clean. And they were there. Nora and Autumn. They had come for me. They always did.

I told them everything. There were tears. So many tears. For Kat. For the years we lost. But for the first time, there was no distance between us. No force keeping us apart. Only a shared, painful, beautiful truth.

We go back to Nora's garage, to talk, to figure out the future of us.

My old camcorder sits on the shelf. I don't need it anymore. Nora's hand is in mine, the love we had is still there, we're going to figure out how to make this work.

Nora tells me how her wife is leaving her, she's unhappy. Some may call it fate, reuniting when we can be together.

Autumn is happy for Nora and I. We're planning a group trip later this year to Seattle, we're going to go to a concert to make up for the one we missed.

We'll never forget Kat, we all keep a piece from the box to carry with us every day to remember her.

The tape ends here. But for the first time in my life, I feel like my story is just beginning..
